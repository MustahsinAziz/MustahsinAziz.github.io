About Me

Hey there! I’m Mustahsin-Ul Aziz, a curious mind with a knack for numbers and a heart set on making an impact. I’m currently a Ph.D. candidate in Natural Resource Economics at West Virginia University, where I spend my days researching how to create sustainable solutions for some of the world’s trickiest environmental issues. Before diving into academia, I worked at the World Bank, where I got to roll up my sleeves and work on projects aimed at transforming education and combating climate challenges across the globe. But there’s more to me than spreadsheets and research papers. When I’m not immersed in data, you’ll find me either plucking away on my bass guitar, perfecting my culinary skills in the kitchen, or exploring new video game worlds. I’ve always believed that good music, great food, and a compelling storyline are the best remedies for a long day! I’m also a big fan of nature, and I love taking long walks by the river—there’s something magical about the combination of fresh air and the gentle murmur of water. Professionally, I’ve managed multi-million dollar projects, led workshops on using AI in research, and even contributed to shaping education policies that impact thousands. On the academic side, my research leverages remote sensing and advanced econometrics to explore how environmental factors influence social outcomes. And when I’m not crunching numbers, I’m usually cooking up new ideas to make a meaningful difference. So whether you’re here to connect on research, exchange cooking tips, or talk about your favorite game, I’m excited to share my story and hear yours!
